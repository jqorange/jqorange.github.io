Jiaqi Shen

Computational neuroscience · Machine learning for behavior & neural data

I work on large-scale behavioral and neural data in freely moving rodents, with a focus on sensor fusion (IMU, video-based pose estimation, UWB) and interpretable models of hippocampal circuits.

Broadly, I am interested in how to build principled models that link neural activity, behavior, and environment in complex, naturalistic tasks.

Affiliation: Cornell University
Fields: Computational neuroscience, machine learning

Research

Selected directions and ongoing work.
  • Multimodal behavior modeling with IMU, DLC and UWB
    2024 – present
    Developing a pipeline that combines inertial measurements, pose estimation and UWB tracking into a unified representation for unsupervised behavior discovery and robust decoding across indoor and outdoor environments.
    Topics: SLDS, self-supervised learning, cross-environment generalization.
  • Hippocampal coding in large environments
    Fernández-Ruiz Lab, Cornell
    Linking behavior segmentation to hippocampal population activity to study how place cells, theta sequences and replay relate to exploration, local search, and goal-directed behavior in meter-scale territories.
    Topics: CA1, replay, theta sequences, behavior segmentation.
