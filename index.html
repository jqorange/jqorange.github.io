About
Short bio

I am currently a Research Assistant in the Fernández-Ruiz and Oliva labs at Cornell University. My work focuses on building multimodal pipelines for rodent behavior, combining IMU, pose estimation, and UWB tracking, and relating them to hippocampal dynamics.

I am broadly interested in behavior analysis, representation learning, and the neural mechanisms of navigation and memory in large, naturalistic environments.

Before coming to Cornell, I received my B.S. in Artificial Intelligence from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, and completed an M.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell.

Behavior pipeline: IMU + DLC + UWB
Python · batch preprocessing · feature engineering

A modular pipeline for loading, synchronizing, and processing IMU, pose (DLC), and UWB tracking into standardized feature tables suitable for large-scale behavior modeling.

Code (private / on request)
Handwritten Chinese classical text recognition
Shanghai Library Open Data Contest · First Prize

Developed a page-segmentation strategy based on the AMPD algorithm and a preprocessing module for PageNet tailored to handwritten Chinese characters, achieving >95% recognition accuracy.

Intelligent vegetable recognition
National Innovation Program

Built a Swin-Transformer-based model and used latent-space interpolation to handle few-sample classes, improving Top-5 accuracy by eight percentage points without augmentation.

Image matching and SfM
CVPR 2023 Image Matching Challenge · Bronze Medal

Investigated feature extraction and matching schemes for robust correspondence and used COLMAP to reconstruct 3D scenes from multi-view images.
